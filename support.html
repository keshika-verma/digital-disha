Volunteer roles

Find where you can contribute.

Each volunteer role is specific. You do not need to do everything. You only need to bring your strengths to the part of Digital Disha where they fit best.

Role 01

Teaching Volunteers

Mode: Offline, unless otherwise specified by the school or organization.

Skills needed

Strong communication and public speaking
Ability to explain concepts patiently and clearly
Basic to intermediate computer science or digital literacy knowledge

What you would do

Assist during computer basics and digital literacy sessions
Help students practice the tools taught during class
Support students one-on-one during activities and projects

Best for

Students, peer educators, confident speakers, and people who enjoy explaining ideas in a simple and friendly way.

Role 02

Curriculum & Content Volunteers

Mode: Online.

Skills needed

Intermediate to strong computer knowledge
Understanding of presentation tools like PowerPoint, Canva, or similar platforms
Ability to create clear lessons for school-going students
Animation skills are a strong plus

What you would do

Create lesson plans, slide decks, worksheets, and practice tasks
Design beginner-friendly activities and mini projects
Help update content for newer digital tools, AI awareness, and online safety

Best for

People who enjoy technology, teaching material, writing, presentations, educational design, and making difficult ideas easier to understand.

Role 03

Design & Media Volunteers

Mode: Online or offline, depending on availability.

Skills needed

Good writing skills for posts, captions, and website content
Ability to create posters, slides, or outreach visuals
Photo, video, reel, or cohort-visual editing skills are a plus

What you would do

Help maintain Digital Disha’s Instagram and LinkedIn presence
Create or edit visuals for outreach, updates, and cohort documentation
Help organize gallery content and maintain a clear visual identity

Best for

Tech or non-tech background students who enjoy design, storytelling, writing, social media, photography, editing, or creative communication.

Role 04

Outreach & Partnerships Volunteers

Mode: Mostly online, with offline possibilities during cohort planning.

Skills needed

Strong communication and coordination
Ability to write good emails and LinkedIn messages
Confidence in contacting schools, organizations, sponsors, or mentors

What you would do

Identify schools, colleges, orphanages, community centers, or youth spaces
Help communicate with teachers, principals, organizations, or sponsors
Draft outreach messages and maintain follow-up records

Best for

High schoolers, college students, organizers, email writers, researchers, and people who enjoy communication and coordination.

What kind of support helps Digital Disha grow?

Sponsorship and partnerships can help Digital Disha run more useful, repeatable, and well-documented cohorts. Support may include funds, tools, resources, logistics, mentorship, connections, or any contribution that helps take digital literacy to more students.

Funding for printing certificates, worksheets, and learning material
Travel and logistics support for future cohorts and volunteers
Laptops, computer access, software tools, or internet resources
Mentorship, guest sessions, or technical guidance
School, NGO, community, or institutional connections
Website, video, documentation, or outreach support
Long-term partnership for rural digital literacy projects
Other support you believe can help the initiative grow
