Team

Started by one, open to many.

Digital Disha began as a one-woman army, built with a laptop, a plan, a classroom, and a belief that practical digital learning should reach students beyond the cities too. Now, the goal is to grow it into a small but serious team of people who care about education, technology, and rural impact.

Founder

Keshika Verma: student, writer, coder, changemaker.

Keshika Verma is the founder of Digital Disha. She comes from Himachal Pradesh and understands the realities of mountain and rural communities closely. Her work sits at the intersection of technology, education, writing, leadership, and social impact.

She completed her education till Grade 10 at Our Lady of the Snows High School, Kullu, one of the leading schools in the valley, and later moved to Chandigarh for Grades 11 and 12. During this time, she strengthened her skills in leadership, communication, technology, and community work, which later helped her launch Digital Disha's first on-site cohort.

As a published author, coder, and young changemaker, Keshika believes that rural students do not lack talent. What they need is access, direction, and the confidence to explore modern tools. Digital Disha is her way of sharing the skills she has built with students who deserve the same chance to understand and create with technology.
