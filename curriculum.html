Curriculum

A curriculum that grows with the digital world.

Digital Disha uses a practical, cohort-based curriculum. It is structured enough to teach real skills, but flexible enough to change with student needs, available resources, and the technologies shaping the world right now.

Structured, but never frozen.

Digital Disha does not follow a rigid one-size-fits-all syllabus forever. Each cohort is shaped around the students in front of us and the digital skills most useful to them.

Our approach

Built for real classrooms, not just paper plans.

Digital Disha's curriculum is designed to be useful, visual, and confidence-building. The focus is not just completing topics, but helping students use digital tools without fear.

01

Practical first

Students learn tools they can actually use: documents, presentations, design, internet search, email, and simple webpages.

02

Hands-on always

Every session is built around trying, making, experimenting, asking questions, and creating real outputs.

03

Evolving with time

Future cohorts can adapt to include AI awareness, digital creativity, responsible internet use, and newer tools.

Cohort 1 day-by-day curriculum

What was taught at Government High School, Kotli.

The first cohort began with computer basics and gradually moved toward creative tools, internet use, and simple web design. The final days focused on practice, revision, projects, and presentations.

Back to Curriculum Archive
Day
1

Essential Computer Skills

What is a computer, keyboard and mouse demo, desktop navigation, file creation, and file management.

Day
2

Word & Data Essentials

MS Word typing, formatting, inserting images, MS Excel tables, and basic math functions.

Day
3

Visual Design Tools

PowerPoint slides, titles, images, Canva poster creation, layout basics, and visual communication.

Day
4

The Internet

What is the internet, browsers, Google Search activities, introduction to Gmail, and online awareness.

Day
5

HTML Web Design I

Structure of a webpage and basic tags including html, body, headings, paragraphs, and writing text.

Day
6

HTML Web Design II

Adding links, images, line breaks, background colors, and basic page layout concepts.

Days
7-10

Practice, Projects & Presentations

Students recreated webpages, styled headings, practiced HTML projects, revised Word, Excel, and PowerPoint tasks, and presented their work.
