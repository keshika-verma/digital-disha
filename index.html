Why Disha?

Disha means direction.

The name Digital Disha reflects the purpose of the initiative: to give rural students a direction into the digital world. “Disha” means direction in Hindi, a language rooted in the communities we work with. Through short workshops, we do not aim to teach everything, but to introduce students to digital skills, spark their interest, and help them see technology as something they can understand, use, and create with.

WHAT CHANGES IN A DIGITAL DISHA WORKSHOP?

The first spark matters. Before skill comes exposure.

Many students in rural classrooms have heard about technology, but rarely get the chance to explore it closely. Digital Disha creates that first spark, helping students touch, try, question, and create with digital tools instead of only watching the digital world from a distance. In a short workshop, students move from hesitation to hands-on confidence... typing their first document, designing a poster, using digital tools, and building a simple webpage. The goal is not to make them experts overnight, but to make technology feel reachable, useful, and exciting. ❤️

Meet the founder

Built by one. Meant for many.

Digital Disha was founded by Keshika Verma, a student, writer, coder, published author, and young changemaker from Himachal Pradesh. Having grown up in the mountains and studied in both Kullu and Chandigarh, she understands the gap between talent and access, especially when it comes to practical technology exposure in rural communities.

Through Digital Disha, she hopes to share the skills she has built in technology, leadership, writing, and community work with students who deserve the same confidence to explore the digital world.
