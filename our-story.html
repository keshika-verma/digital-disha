Our Story

Where access is limited, opportunity should not be.

Digital Disha began with a simple concern: many students in rural India still grow up with limited exposure to the digital skills shaping today’s world. The initiative was started to bridge that gap by bringing basic computer learning, creative tools, and early coding exposure into classrooms where such opportunities are often out of reach.

The question

Who gets to feel ready for the digital world?

Digital Disha began with a simple thought: digital learning should not feel distant, delayed, or out of reach for any student. In a country moving rapidly toward technology, students need more than awareness of computers, they need early, hands-on exposure that helps them feel familiar with the digital world.

Having received strong digital education herself, Keshika Verma wanted to share that starting point with students who may not get the same exposure as early or as practically. Digital Disha grew from that intention: to make digital confidence begin sooner.

The preparation

Skills became more meaningful when they could be shared.

After studying in Kullu till Grade 10, Keshika moved to Chandigarh for Grades 11 and 12. There, she took on leadership roles, explored technology and writing more seriously, worked with organizations, and learned how ideas become real projects.

Digital Disha became the place where those experiences came together. The goal was not to deliver a lecture and leave. The goal was to create a classroom where students could touch the keyboard, try the tools, make mistakes, build something, and realize that technology was not distant from them.

The first classroom

The idea became real at Government High School, Kotli.

Digital Disha officially came to life at Government High School, Kotli, in Mandi district, Himachal Pradesh. The first cohort was a 10-day program beginning June 16, 2025, with 24 students from classes 9 and 10. Keshika was still a 12th grader at that time.

The sessions introduced students to computer basics, digital tools, internet safety, creative design, and simple web development. Students learned through live demonstrations, visual explanations, and hands-on practice. They created documents, posters, presentations, and web pages, building comfort with technology step by step.

What changed

Not just a workshop. A first push.

The most meaningful part of Digital Disha was not only the curriculum. It was watching students gain confidence as they explored tools, asked questions, and created their own work. A computer lab can be present in a school, but confidence only grows when students are encouraged to use it without fear.

The first cohort received support from educators, including a letter of recommendation from Headmaster Mr. Nihal Singh, and recognition from leaders such as Dr. Kiran Bedi. More importantly, it showed that a student-led initiative could create a real starting point for digital learning in rural classrooms.

The road ahead

One school was the start, not the destination.

Digital Disha began in one classroom, but the vision is much larger. The goal is to take digital literacy to more government schools, orphanages, foundling homes, and children’s homes, especially in mountain and underserved regions.

The dream is to build a program that is practical, flexible, and rooted in real student needs. As technology keeps changing, Digital Disha will continue to evolve, bringing students the skills and confidence they need for the world ahead.

Talent exists everywhere. Opportunity should too.

Digital Disha is a promise to keep building work that is practical, rooted, and useful for the students it is meant to serve.
